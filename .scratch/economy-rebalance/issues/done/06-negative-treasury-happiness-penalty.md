# Negative-treasury daily happiness penalty (-0.05 flat)

Status: ready-for-agent
Category: enhancement

## Parent

[PRD: Economy & balance pass](../PRD.md)

## What to build

Apply a flat daily happiness penalty of -0.05 whenever the end-of-day treasury is negative, so that running broke has a population consequence beyond the cash crunch. The penalty is flat (no scaling with depth or duration) and disappears the day treasury returns non-negative — no accumulation, no death spiral, recovery always possible.

The penalty is subtracted after existing happiness terms and before the [0, 1.5] clamp in the population module. The check runs against `state.treasury` at the time happiness is computed for the next day's growth velocity.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Treasury closes positive → no penalty applied; happiness matches today's behavior.
- [ ] Treasury closes negative → happiness drops by exactly 0.05 (before the [0, 1.5] clamp).
- [ ] Treasury goes from negative to non-negative on the next day → penalty is gone immediately.
- [ ] Penalty does not scale with treasury depth or duration of negativity.
- [ ] New tests in `world/tests/test_population.py` (or equivalent) cover the three cases above.
- [ ] `make check` passes.

## Agent Brief

**Category:** enhancement
**Summary:** Apply a flat -0.05 happiness penalty whenever the day closes with a negative treasury, with instant recovery once treasury crosses non-negative.

**Current behavior:**
A negative treasury has no civic consequence. The cash crunch is recoverable indefinitely with no impact on the city.

**Desired behavior:**
On each day's happiness computation, after all existing happiness terms and before the [0, 1.5] clamp, subtract exactly 0.05 if treasury is currently negative. The penalty is flat (does not scale with depth or duration of negativity) and disappears the day treasury returns to non-negative. No accumulation, no death spiral, recovery always possible.

**Key interfaces:**
- The happiness pipeline in the population module — a new conditional subtraction of 0.05 sits after existing terms and before the clamp.
- The treasury value read by this term — the current state's treasury at the time happiness is computed for the next day's growth velocity.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Treasury non-negative → no penalty; happiness matches today's behavior.
- [ ] Treasury negative → exactly 0.05 is subtracted before the [0, 1.5] clamp.
- [ ] The penalty does not scale with treasury magnitude or with how long treasury has been negative.
- [ ] A transition from negative to non-negative removes the penalty immediately on the next happiness computation.
- [ ] `make check` passes.

**Out of scope:**
- Depth- or duration-scaled penalties (explicitly rejected by the PRD).
- New happiness terms unrelated to treasury.
- Bankruptcy or game-over mechanics.
